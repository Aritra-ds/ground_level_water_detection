Precautions to Preserve Groundwater

Simple steps to help conserve and protect our precious groundwater resources.

Take Action Today!

Use Native Plants

Native plants require less water and fertilizer, reducing the strain on groundwater and promoting a healthy ecosystem.

Limit Chemical Use

Avoid using chemicals around your home, as they can contaminate groundwater and harm the ecosystem.

Proper Waste Disposal

Dispose of toxic substances like chemicals and pharmaceuticals properly to prevent contamination of groundwater sources.

Avoid Water Wastage

Be mindful of your water usage. Turn off taps when not in use to prevent unnecessary wastage of valuable resources.

Fix Leaks

Leaking pipes and fixtures waste water. Fix leaks promptly to conserve water and reduce unnecessary groundwater depletion.

Limit Shower Time

Taking shorter showers helps reduce water consumption, conserving both water and the energy used to heat it.

Water Plants Smartly

Water plants only when necessary and during cooler times of the day to minimize water loss due to evaporation.

Reduce Waste

Recycling reduces the amount of waste that ends up in landfills, which helps reduce contamination of groundwater supplies.

Use Non-Toxic Cleaners

Choose eco-friendly, non-toxic cleaning products to prevent harmful chemicals from entering the water supply.

Educate Others

Share your knowledge about water conservation to raise awareness and inspire action to preserve groundwater.
